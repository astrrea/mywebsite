What are Constellations?

Constellations is a group of stars that forms an imaginary pattern in the night sky. These stars form particular shapes if you draw imaginary lines like a dot-to-dot puzzle(Nasa, 2021).

The most common constellations in the sky

1. Ursa Major - also known as the great bear, it is ladle shaped and found in the northern night sky. 2. Orion - also known as the great hunter, it is visible from all parts of the world as it is located in the celestial equator. 3. Cassiopeia - named after mother of Andromeda, Queen Cassiopeia.
It is composed of five very bright stars that forms the shape of a W. It is located in the northen hemisphere. References: Starwalk.space

How are constellations beneficial?

Star constellations are beneficial because astronauts used them as landmarks in the sky. Stars are in a fixed location and approximately in the same spot for plenty of years.
In the ancient times, they used constellations to create a calendar for harvests.
